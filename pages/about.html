WEC

Click to expand details

The FIA World Endurance Championship (WEC) is the world's premier international sports car racing series. Instead of short sprints like Formula 1, WEC is all about endurance, testing mechanical reliability, fuel efficiency, and driver stamina over grueling long-distance races.

Here is what makes the championship unique:

  • The Multi-Class Format: Cars from different performance tiers race on the track at the very same time. You have the bleeding-edge, high-downforce Hypercars battling for the overall win while simultaneously navigating traffic around the production-based LMGT3 sports cars.
  • The Races: Event lengths typically range from 6 to 10 hours, held at iconic circuits worldwide like Spa-Francorchamps, Fuji, and Interlagos.
  • The Crown Jewel: The centerpiece of the WEC calendar is the legendary 24 Hours of Le Mans in France, an ultimate 24-hour test of human and machine engineering.
  • Teamwork: Every car is shared by a lineup of three drivers who swap out during pit stops, making strategy, traffic management, and consistency just as vital as outright speed.

Formula One

Click to expand details

The FIA Formula One World Championship (F1) is the highest class of international racing for open-wheel, single-seater formula cars. Unlike the multi-class endurance testing of WEC, F1 is a hyper-focused sprint series built entirely around raw speed, cutting-edge aerodynamics, and ultimate driver precision.

Here is what makes the championship unique:

  • The Single-Class Sprint: Every driver on the grid is racing in identical categories of machinery, fighting for the exact same track position. There are no slower production classes to lap — just 20 of the fastest cars in the world fighting wheel-to-wheel in a high-speed sprint that typically lasts just 90 minutes.
  • The Races (Grands Prix): Events are held across a massive global calendar, featuring a mix of legendary permanent circuits like Silverstone and Monza, alongside tight, unforgiving street tracks like Monaco, Singapore, and Las Vegas.
  • The Crown Jewel: While there isn't a single standalone endurance race, the Monaco Grand Prix is widely considered the ultimate test of precision, where drivers skim barriers at incredible speeds on a circuit with virtually zero margin for error.
  • Individual & Team Battle: Among the 10 teams that design and build their own custom chassis and power units to win the Constructors' Championship, the ultimate glory goes to the individual. Two drivers per team compete fiercely against the rest of the grid — and each other — for the Drivers' World Championship.
